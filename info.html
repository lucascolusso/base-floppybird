Flappy bird study information form

Welcome to Flappy Bird casual game experiment. Participation in the study typically takes 20 minutes and is strictly anonymous. Participants are requested to play several rounds of the game, as much as they want.


After that, participants answer a series of questions about their personal beliefs in executing tasks and how they compare to others, along with demographics and personality surveys. All responses are confidential, and in no case will responses from individual participants be identified. All data will be pooled and published in aggregate form only. The study involves no more than minimal risk to participants (i.e., the level of risk encountered in daily life). Many individuals find participation in this study enjoyable, and no adverse reactions have been reported. Participation is voluntary, refusal to take part in the study involves no penalty or loss of benefits to which participants are otherwise entitled.


This study is being conducted by Ph.D. student Lucas Franco Colusso and Professors Sean Munson and Gary Hsieh of the University of Washington. If participants have further questions about this study or their rights, or if they wish to lodge a complaint or concern, they may contact the principal investigator, student Lucas Franco Colusso, at (206) 319-8895 or colusso@uw.edu. If you are 18 years of age or older, understand the statements above, and freely consent to participate in the study, click on the "I agree to participate!" button to begin the experiment.
